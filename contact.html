Why this page exists

This page is here to practice having a clear contact section on a simple site. The main goal is making the structure easy to understand through headings and paragraphs.

Since GitHub Pages doesn’t process form submissions by itself, I’m not building an actual form on this site right now. Instead, I’m describing what I would include if forms were enabled.

Even without a form, a contact page still matters because it gives users a clear place to look when they want to reach the site owner.

What a form would collect

If a form worked on GitHub Pages

If this site included a form, it would collect a name, an email address, and a short message. That would be enough for someone to contact me about feedback or questions.

I would also include an optional dropdown for the reason for contacting me, like “class project,” “feedback,” or “collaboration,” so messages are easier to sort.

A form would make sense because it gives people a direct way to communicate without leaving the site, and it keeps the contact process clear and simple.
